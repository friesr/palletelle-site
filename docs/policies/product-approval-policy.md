# Product Approval Policy

## Purpose

Define who can approve products, when to escalate to Rodney, and how to keep the review queue focused on genuinely uncertain items.

## Approval authority

Delegated reviewers may make the decision for clear cases.

Escalate to Rodney only when the item is genuinely uncertain, borderline, or conflicts with the policy.

## Hard exclusions

These are automatic fails and do not need Rodney review unless the product data or imagery is unclear:

- no mini-skirts
- skirts must be at least midi / mid length
- no short-shorts
- nothing cheeky

## Operational handling

### Clear pass

Approve directly when the product clearly fits the brand and does not trigger any exclusion.

Actions:
- mark approved
- remove from review queue
- do not send to Rodney

### Clear fail

Reject directly when the product clearly violates a hard exclusion.

Actions:
- mark rejected
- remove from review queue
- do not send to Rodney, unless the listing is ambiguous and needs clarification

### Borderline or uncertain

Escalate only when the reviewer cannot confidently classify the item from the available product data.

Examples:
- hem length is unclear from images or copy
- cut or styling may read as cheeky but is not obvious
- conflicting photos, metadata, or merchandising context

Actions:
- hold for Rodney review
- include the specific reason the item is borderline
- keep the escalation note short and decision-oriented

## Queue policy

The review queue should stay small by routing clear-pass and clear-fail items out immediately.

Only borderline or uncertain products should remain in the Rodney-facing approval queue.

## Rodney-facing approvals

Rodney should see exception handling, not routine approvals.

Send Rodney only:
- borderline items
- unclear or conflicting listings
- cases where policy interpretation is genuinely uncertain

Do not send Rodney batches of obvious passes or obvious fails.
