# Launch catalog execution brief - 2026-04-21

## Objective

Populate the launch store with a small, survivable starter catalog that reflects Atelier's modesty standard without creating a large Rodney review burden.

## Operating decision

Commerce, intake, validation, and operations own first-pass approval for clear cases.

Escalate to Rodney only when an item is genuinely uncertain on modesty, source truth, or launch suitability.

## Hard policy to enforce immediately

Reject or hold any item with any of the following:
- mini-skirt length
- skirts shorter than midi / mid length
- short-shorts
- cheeky presentation
- obviously clingy silhouettes
- overtly flirtatious silhouettes
- necklines, slits, sheerness, or fit that make the item depend on rescue styling to become acceptable

Default rule: if modesty depends on layering tricks, special posing, or generous interpretation, it is not a clear-approve item.

## Launch assortment shape

Start narrow and stable:
- dresses first
- a few skirts second
- a very small number of layering pieces only where they support the dress/skirt assortment
- avoid novelty, trend-chasing, or fragile marketplace clutter

Target launch mix:
- 6 to 10 clear hero dresses
- 2 to 4 clear skirts
- 1 to 3 layering/support pieces
- 0 borderline items published at launch

## Team workflow

### 1) Intake
Intake gathers candidates only from sources with enough evidence to review:
- live PDP fetch when possible
- otherwise strong indexed snippet evidence with explicit caveats
- preserve URL, retrieval date, source facts, and what remains unknown

Intake should pre-filter out:
- short hems
- body-skimming knits or denim
- weak product photography
- low-information listings
- unstable or already-unavailable product pages

### 2) Commerce first-pass review
Commerce assigns one of three outcomes:
- **Advance**: clearly compliant, on-brand, and launch-useful
- **Hold for verification**: likely usable, but missing source proof or visuals
- **Escalate**: true ambiguity on modesty, silhouette, or category fit

Commerce should approve without Rodney when all are true:
- hem is clearly midi or longer
- silhouette reads loose, flared, tiered, or otherwise non-clingy
- neckline and coverage are clearly safe
- source confidence is usable enough for staging
- item improves launch balance

### 3) Validation
Validation confirms:
- normalized source facts stay separate from editorial judgment
- price and availability are treated as freshness-sensitive
- missing attributes are listed explicitly
- staged items are tagged by confidence and next verification step

Validation should downgrade or hold when:
- source is snippet-only and visuals are incomplete
- length language conflicts with imagery
- fabric may be sheer or clingy
- item is indexed but live availability is unproven

### 4) Operations
Operations manages the launch queue:
- publish only the highest-confidence products first
- keep a reserve bench of medium-confidence replacements
- refresh source availability before any customer-visible activation
- withdraw quickly if source health degrades

## Escalation rules

Escalate to Rodney only for these cases:
- neckline or silhouette is borderline rather than clearly safe
- hem is labeled midi but appears knee-length or ambiguous
- straight or jersey fabric may read clingy
- product is strategically important but evidence is incomplete
- category policy needs interpretation beyond the current rules

Do not escalate clear rejects.
Do not escalate obvious approvals.
Use Rodney for judgment calls, not bulk screening.
