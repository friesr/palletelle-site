## Borderline-item rules for this modesty-first affiliate model

Use these rules when an item is not obviously out, but not clearly safe.

### 1) Modesty is judged visually, not by title language alone
- "Modest," "midi," "maxi," or "church" in the title is supportive evidence, not proof.
- Any item with weak photos or ambiguous silhouette stays out.

### 2) Coverage must be native, not rescued
- Prefer items that are modest on their own.
- If a piece only works with a camisole, slip, underskirt, or strategic crop of the photo, treat it as borderline at best.
- If rescue layering is essential, do not make it a lead product.

### 3) Reject body-skimming shapes even when lengths are conservative
- Long or midi length does not offset cling.
- Pencil, straight knit, thin jersey, or denim pieces should be rejected when they define hips, seat, or bust too clearly.

### 4) Hem and neckline ambiguity default to no
- If one photo looks knee length and another looks midi, assume the weaker case until proven.
- If the neckline might dip, gap, or depend on pose, it is not strong enough.

### 5) Fabric opacity matters as much as cut
- Thin cotton, slub knits, jersey, and light colors need extra scrutiny.
- Any item that risks translucency in daylight should be excluded.

### 6) Affiliate durability matters
- In this model, weak catalog stability is itself a product-quality issue.
- Marketplace fashion listings that churn quickly should lose to stable brand/store pages or durable catalog basics, even when the style is acceptable.

### 7) Editorial standard matters
- Passing modesty is necessary, not sufficient.
- Keep out pieces that are merely acceptable but visually generic, flimsy, or off-brand for a feminine, traditional, modest assortment.
