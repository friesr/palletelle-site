# Color specialist network execution plan - 2026-04-22

## Purpose

Translate the monetization brief into a practical launch plan for a curated Palletelle color specialist network.

This plan assumes Palletelle will launch as a **Palletelle-managed expert network**, not an open marketplace, with manual operations supporting the first release.

## Executive recommendation

Launch a **curated pilot network** with:
- standardized session products
- fixed launch pricing
- guided specialist matching rather than open directory behavior
- strong manual approval, support, and QA controls
- a starting Palletelle take rate of **25%** on standard bookings

Do not wait for a fully automated marketplace before go-to-market work begins. Marketing can start validating demand, building waitlist intent, recruiting expert supply, and pre-selling the service promise now.

## What marketing can start now, before platform completion

Marketing does not need the final booking stack to begin. The immediate goal is to prove demand, shape positioning, and build a launch pipeline.

### Start immediately
- publish messaging around **live color guidance from vetted Palletelle specialists**
- create a waitlist landing page for customers interested in live sessions
- create a specialist interest/application landing page
- test customer demand with email, social, quiz-result CTAs, and in-product banners
- recruit the first cohort of specialists manually through outreach
- run customer discovery interviews with likely buyers
- run pricing tests for 15 to 20 minute consults and 45 to 60 minute core sessions
- collect early use cases: palette confusion, shopping decisions, wardrobe refresh, occasion styling, follow-up interpretation
- build launch content showing what a live session helps with and what deliverables a customer gets
- start an internal or concierge-style pilot using manual intake plus manual scheduling

### Pre-platform marketing assets to produce now
- landing page copy for customer waitlist
- landing page copy for specialist applications
- FAQ covering what the service is and is not
- launch email sequence for interested customers
- specialist recruiting deck or one-pager
- short-form content themes: palette confusion, confidence, fit-for-occasion color choices, why live help matters
- founder or brand point of view on why Palletelle is curating experts instead of opening a generic marketplace

### Early validation targets
- waitlist signups
- specialist applications from qualified candidates
- CTA clickthrough from quiz/product surfaces
- manual pilot conversion rate from inquiry to booked session
- willingness-to-pay signals by session type
- most requested outcomes and objections

## Immediate deliverables, next 2 to 4 weeks

## 1. Service design
Define the initial offer set and customer promise.

### Deliverables
- service catalog with **2 to 4 standardized session types**
- draft customer-facing positioning and service descriptions
- fixed launch pricing recommendation
- clear deliverables per session type
- policy draft for cancellation, rescheduling, no-shows, refunds

### Recommended launch session set
- **Quick consult**: 15 to 20 minutes, fast confidence check or targeted question
- **Core color session**: 45 minutes, live color guidance and interpretation
- **Premium review session**: 60 minutes, live session plus recap notes or recommendations
- **Follow-up mini session**: short paid follow-up for shopping, wardrobe, or clarification

## 2. Supply recruitment and quality gate
Build the first launch-ready specialist bench.

### Deliverables
- specialist application form
- review rubric for credentials, communication, and sample work
- mock session rubric
- provider agreement draft
- specialist onboarding checklist
- approval state model: Applied, Vetted, Calibrated, Launch-approved, Trusted, Suspended
- first cohort target list and outreach sequence

### Immediate execution target
Recruit and calibrate a small initial bench of specialists with enough timezone and language coverage for the pilot, even if only 3 to 8 are truly launch-ready.

## 3. Concierge pilot operations
Stand up the service manually before full product automation.

### Deliverables
- internal SOP for customer intake
- manual matching workflow
- manual scheduling workflow
- support workflow for booking changes and disputes
- post-session feedback form
- session recap template
- admin tracker for bookings, specialist status, refunds, and notes

### Recommended manual stack for pilot
- landing page or intake form
- manual email confirmation flow
- calendar scheduling tool
- approved video meeting tool
- spreadsheet or lightweight admin tracker for booking state and payout tracking

## 4. Product definition for staging
Define the minimum platform footprint required to move from concierge ops to staging.

### Deliverables
- specialist profile data model
- booking and availability data model
- booking state machine
- wireframes for booking flow and specialist profile
- admin requirements for approval, suspension, refunds, and dispute notes
- event tracking plan for booking funnel and post-session outcomes

## 5. Monetization and finance rules
Make the economics and edge-case rules explicit before launch.

### Deliverables
- launch pricing sheet
- take-rate policy
- payout timing policy
- refund allocation rules
- promo and discount handling rules
- reconciliation process owner

## Staged rollout

## Phase 0, execution prep
**Goal:** finalize offer, policy, and operating model.

### Exit criteria
- service catalog approved
- pricing approved
- policy drafts complete
- provider agreement draft complete
- specialist application and review rubrics ready
- manual pilot workflow owner assigned

## Phase 1, concierge pilot
**Goal:** validate demand and service quality with manual support.

### What ships
- customer waitlist / intake page
- specialist application page
- manual scheduling and payment workflow
- manual matching by Palletelle
- first vetted specialists in limited coverage

### Success signals
- meaningful inquiry-to-booking conversion
- strong attendance and satisfaction
- low refund/dispute volume
- repeat or follow-up booking intent
- specialists can deliver consistent outcomes inside Palletelle standards

### Recommended constraints
- limited geographies or timezones
- fixed pricing only
- limited number of session types
- no open browsing sprawl

## Phase 2, staged product release
**Goal:** replace fragile manual steps with productized workflows while keeping curation and controls.

### What ships
- on-platform specialist profiles
- guided matching or recommended specialist flow
- availability display and slot selection
- checkout and booking confirmation
- reminder notifications
- admin tooling for refunds, overrides, notes, and suspension

### Success signals
- operational load decreases without hurting trust
- booking funnel conversion remains healthy
- specialist response and quality standards hold
- unit economics remain positive after support and refund costs

## Phase 3, controlled network expansion
**Goal:** grow supply and improve autonomy without becoming a generic marketplace.

### What ships
- bounded specialist self-serve profile editing
- bounded pricing tiers or controlled flexibility
- stronger ratings and quality controls
- improved rebooking and follow-up journeys

### Gate to expand
Only expand after Palletelle proves:
- consistent quality across specialists
- acceptable contribution margin
- support burden is manageable
- customer trust is strengthened, not diluted

## Monetization recommendation

## Recommended model
Launch with **per-session commission** as the core model.

### Recommendation
- Palletelle sets launch pricing or narrow pricing bands
- Palletelle owns the customer booking and trust layer
- specialists are paid after the completion/refund window closes
- Palletelle starts with a **25% take rate** on standard network bookings

### Why this is the right launch choice
- easiest model for customers and specialists to understand
- aligned to actual demand rather than charging supply upfront
- supports manual ops, support, refund handling, and brand packaging
- preserves tighter merchandising and trust than free-form expert pricing

### Not recommended at launch
- open specialist self-pricing
- subscription fees for specialists before lead quality is proven
- off-platform lead-gen as the primary model
- broad open marketplace positioning

## Recommended launch KPIs
- landing page conversion to waitlist
- specialist application completion rate
- inquiry-to-booking conversion
- attendance rate
- completion rate
- refund rate
- CSAT or NPS
- rebook rate
- specialist utilization
- contribution margin per session

## Key risks and execution controls

### Main risks to manage
- inconsistent specialist quality
- customer confusion about what a session delivers
- operational overload from manual support
- insufficient specialist coverage
- weak willingness to pay
- off-platform leakage
- compliance and payout complexity
- brand dilution from marketplace creep

### Controls
- curated supply only
- fixed launch offers and pricing
- manual approval for every specialist
- review of first sessions and feedback
- strong provider agreement and platform policy
- limited scope by geography/timezone if necessary
- guided matching over browse-heavy discovery

## Bottom line

Palletelle should move now with a **curated, manually supported pilot** and treat this as a high-trust premium service layer.

The right execution path is:
- start marketing and specialist recruitment immediately
- validate demand through waitlists and concierge pilots before full platform completion
- build only the minimum required booking and admin infrastructure for staged rollout
- launch with fixed offers and an initial **25% commission model**
- expand only after quality, support load, and economics are proven
